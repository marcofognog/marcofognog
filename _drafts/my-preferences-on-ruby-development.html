I like to have a clean and tested code base, but I am also very pragmatic when coding, chosing explicity over implicty. In most cases, I like pure Ruby rather than DSL's.

I am very flexible about what libries, technologies and methodolgies I work with, but the following are my preferences.

For testing, I like test unit, or the rspec runner (but not much fan of the rspec DSL and matchers), capybara. Also, Simplecov or similar is good to track the coverage.

No SASS ,HAML, or coffescript, but pure CSS, HTML and javascript.

Those are my preferences, wich doesn't mean I can't work with other technoligies (e.g. HAML).

How I do testing:

In general web applications, I don't do unit tests on model, in the most strict sense of the word. But rather, I test the interface of the models (public methods) , and then, if necessary, the private methods. That works good, because I work backwards, first the writing tests about how that object should behave and, then, all private methods are born inside that public method as I refactor it. Technicaly, its a integration test from the model down to the database.

I aslo like very much to do end-to-end tests with capybara. Cucumber only if there's a business guy willing to write and read that.

But there's one thing I can't work with, the mockist style of testing. I find it quite unexpressive, highly coupled to the implementation and brittle. Just not worth mantaining.
